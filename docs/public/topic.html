DEEPFAKE DETECTION

The concept of “Deepfake” was introduced to categorize the manipulation and alteration of audio or visual media through the application of advanced deep learning algorithms. The term first emerged in 2017, popularized by Reddit users, and has since witnessed a rapid and widespread evolution. In just a few years, deepfakes have become increasingly sophisticated and accessible, enabling the creation of highly convincing fake content that can be difficult to distinguish from authentic material. This technological advancement, while impressive, poses significant concerns due to its potential to mislead audiences, spread misinformation, and undermine trust in digital media. As a result, deepfakes are now regarded as a substantial threat, particularly because of their ability to make false information appear convincingly real, raising serious ethical, social, and security challenges.

Nowadays, we have two types of tools to identify deepfakes; Detection and authentication methods. The first employ ML (machine learning) models, specially deep neural networks, to assess whether the media piece presents any inconsistencies. This method does not require comparison with the original piece. Authentication methods rely on embedded watermarks or metadata, being the absence of them a decisive proof. However, these technologies are not perfect and still need to overcome some challenges such as emerging more powerful deepfake technologies, limited accuracy and computational efficiency.

As for ethical implications, deepfake detection raises privacy and freedom concerns and some issues related to accuracy. For deepfake detection tools to work, they need to train with real data, which sometimes has to be extracted from users. Some deepfakes are not harmful, as they have entertainment purposes, therefore limiting them could be seen as an attack to freedom of speech. Since deepfake technologies are not perfect, we still need to determine how trustful we are with them. All this and much more will be analysed in depth in this essay in order to come to a conclusion about these technologies.

In conclusion, it is important to remark that this new branch of technology poses both an opportunity and a challenge to all of society, from individuals to the system as a whole. On one hand, it has the ability to revolutionise most of the sectors that involve education, and entertainment, but on the other hand, its misuse can lead to a chain of events that will end up reflecting serious consequences in the future. For these reasons, it is essential that these technologies have robust and accurate detection technologies which must be continuously updated to keep pace with the evolving techniques used to create deepfakes. Last but not least, the ethical implications of deepfake technology cannot be overlooked, meaning that a balance between protecting individuals and society from harmful deepfakes and safeguarding freedom of expression will be crucial.
